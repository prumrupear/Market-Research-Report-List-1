<p><p>Ultraviolet light sensors are categorized into three main types based on their wavelength: UVA, UVB, and UVC. UVA sensors detect long-wave UV radiation, primarily linked to skin aging and deeper skin penetration. UVB sensors measure medium-wave UV radiation, associated with sunburn and skin cancer risks. UVC sensors monitor short-wave UV radiation, known for its germicidal properties, often used in sterilization applications. Each type plays a crucial role in health monitoring, environmental studies, and industrial processes, driving demand in their respective markets.</p></p>
