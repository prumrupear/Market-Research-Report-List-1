<p><p>Waste-to-Energy (WTE) refers to the conversion of non-recyclable waste materials into usable energy, often through processes like incineration and anaerobic digestion. Incineration involves combusting waste at high temperatures, generating heat that can be transformed into electricity or steam. Other WTE technologies include anaerobic digestion, which breaks down organic waste to produce biogas, and gasification, which converts waste into synthetic gas. These methods not only reduce landfill volume but also provide sustainable energy sources, contributing to circular economy initiatives.</p></p>
