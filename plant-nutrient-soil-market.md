<p><p>The plant nutrient soil market can be categorized into three types based on pH levels: acidic, alkaline, and neutral. Acidic soils, with a pH below 6, often require lime treatments to enhance nutrient availability for crops. Alkaline soils, above pH 7, may need sulfur or organic matter to improve nutrient uptake. Neutral soils, with a pH around 6 to 7, are generally ideal for most plants, providing a balanced environment for nutrient absorption and supporting diverse agricultural practices.</p></p>
