<p><p>The airport solar panels market primarily features two types: monocrystalline and polysilicon. Monocrystalline solar panels are made from single crystal structures, offering higher efficiency and space savings, making them ideal for limited airport rooftops. In contrast, polysilicon panels consist of multiple silicon crystals, providing a cost-effective solution with slightly lower efficiency. Airports may choose between these types based on budget, space constraints, and energy output requirements, aiming to enhance sustainability and reduce operational costs through renewable energy sources.</p></p>
